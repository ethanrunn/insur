Tenant's insurance can be used by a renter as property insurance to replace lost or stolen items. Liability coverage can also be provided by tenants' insurance to safeguard the renter in the event that they are sued for causing damage while renting an apartment.

The landlord's homeowners' insurance policy does not cover renters. The landlord's property would be covered by the landlord's insurance in the event of a loss, but not the renter's. Therefore, in the event of a fire or burglary, the renter would be obligated to pay for the replacement of any property or possessions.

It is also possible to sue renters for their actions. For instance, if they fail to shut off the bathtub faucet and flood the apartment below, the tenant can be sued by the person below. The renter would be protected in such circumstances if they had tenants' insurance.
