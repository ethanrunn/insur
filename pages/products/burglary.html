What It Means

We'll cover you if your valuables or properties are burgled

Simply put, burglary occurs when a person (or group of people) unlawfully and willfully enters another person's property using force, even if they do not ultimately steal anything.
The following losses are covered:
  • Theft from a closed location such as a vehicle, home, or place of business.
  • The damage caused by the intruder during the course of the burglary.
BREAKING IT DOWN

Despite the fact that the terms "burglary," "robbery," and "theft" are frequently

used interchangeably in everyday speech, the legal and insurance contexts are vastly different. The insurance industry uses a nuanced definition of burglary, which is defined as theft when force is used to unlawfully enter another person's property.

"Theft" is the broadest risk associated with this kind of loss. Any method of taking something without the owner's consent is considered theft, according to insurance terms. Therefore, if theft is listed as an insured peril on your policy, you can rest assured that your property is adequately protected against any loss caused by theft, robbery, or any other type of crime.
