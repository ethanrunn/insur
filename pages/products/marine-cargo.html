When your goods are transported as cargo from one location to another, marine cargo insurance covers the risks of loss, damage, cost, and liability. For instance, from a inland factory to a seaport and then across the ocean to the overseas address of the buyer of your goods.

Airfreight, ocean freight, and overland carriage are all forms of transportation. The purpose of marine cargo insurance is to protect the owners of the cargo and/or financiers like banks from financial loss resulting from physical loss, damage, costs incurred, or liability arising from the transportation process.

Manufacturing and construction companies, importers, corporate organizations, and any business that imports goods by sea or air are all eligible to purchase ABCD's Marine Cargo insurance.

Owners of cargo who transport goods by air or sea include:
Importers and exporters, construction firms, logistics firms, freight forwarders, commodities traders, public institutions, corporate organizations, banks, and small and medium-sized enterprises

The perils of the sea, such as rough weather, sinking, the ports, and overturning, collision, and/or theft from overland transports, can all result in loss.

ABCD's Marine Cargo Insurance protects the owner of the cargo against what is typically a significant capital loss. The cover can be set up for just one shipment or it can be used as an open cover for all shipments in a year. It can likewise be organized to cover either absolute misfortunes or fractional misfortunes.
