Marine hull insurance shields the insured vessel or fleet from physical damage resulting from a sea or other covered peril while it is in transit over water. The policy is important because it helps boat owners protect their vehicles from machinery damage and the loss of hull, fittings, liabilities, and other items.

A clause in our policy called "collision liability" shields the owner of the vessel from legal responsibility in the event that their vessel collides with another ship and damages its cargo or property.

The policy can also be extended to cover legal liability for damage to fixed installations or bodily injury or death. You will need to buy protection and indemnity coverage if you want to get insurance against this kind of liability.

This policy is made to cover owners of watercrafts like boats, ships, yachts, fishing boats, steamers, and so on. The vessel's body, also known as its hull, is the subject of this insurance policy. In the event that the risks covered by the policy cause damage to the vessel's body (hull) or machinery, the insurance provides financial protection.

The following dangers are covered by ABCD Marine Hull Insurance:

Fire
Jettison
Piracy

Malicious acts
Theft of the entire vessel or her boats
Loss of or damage to the subject-matter insured (with some exceptions)
Human negligence (with some exceptions)
